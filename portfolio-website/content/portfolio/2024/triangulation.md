## Overview

This was a **BYU ME EN 101 design project**. The goal was to measure the height of the Provo Temple, including the angel Moroni statue, relative to the west gate.

Our method established a coordinate system with three points: the top of the angel Moroni statue, the west gate of the temple, and a third location approximately 200 feet from the gate. By measuring the angles between vectors from two ground points, we used vector addition in three dimensions to estimate the height.

## Timeline

- **Duration:** February 2024
- **Total time:** 15 hours

## Results

To determine the cosine angles of the vectors, we designed an instrument using two equal-length PVC pipes, a protractor, a small weight, and a compass. The hanging weight ensured alignment relative to the ground, while the protractor and compass captured the needed angle measurements.
